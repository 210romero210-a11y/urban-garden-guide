# Apartment Composting 101

**You can turn kitchen scraps into rich compost without leaving your apartment.** Whether you're on a 15th-floor apartment or a studio with no balcony, there's a composting method that works for you.

The key is choosing the right system for your space and lifestyle. This guide covers three methods that apartment gardeners successfully use.

---

## Why Compost in Your Apartment?

### Benefits

- **Reduce waste** - Kitchen scraps are 30% of typical household trash
- **Free fertilizer** - Rich nutrients for your herbs and vegetables
- **No smell** - Proper systems are completely odor-free
- **Satisfying** - Close the loop on your food

### What You Can Compost

| Composts Easily | Needs Caution |
|---------------|--------------|
| Fruit & vegetable scraps | Coffee grounds |
| Coffee filters (unbleached) | Tea bags (unbleached) |
| Eggshells | Bread/grains (sparingly) |
| Fruit peels | Dairy (bokashi only) |
| Plant trimmings | Meat/fats (bokashi only) |

## Method 1: Worm Farming (Most Popular)

Vermicomposting uses red wiggler worms to process your scraps. It's odor-free, compact, and produces incredibly rich castings.

### Pros
- Odor-free when maintained properly
- Produces liquid fertilizer ("worm tea")
- Can be stored under sink or on counter
- Processes most kitchen scraps
- Educational for kids

### Cons
- Requires purchasing worms
- Need to manage temperature (65-80°F ideal)
- Requires some maintenance weekly
- Can't handle citrus/bulb onion in large quantities

### Getting Started

**Supplies Needed:**
- [Worm Bin](https://www.amazon.com) - $30-50
- [Red Wigglers](https://www.amazon.com) - 1 lb ($25-40)
- [Bedding](https://www.amazon.com) - Shredded newspaper/cardboard
- [Coconut coir](https://www.amazon.com) - Worm bedding

**Basic Setup:**
1. Prepare bedding (damp shredded newspaper)
2. Add worms (wait 1 day after setting up bin)
3. Bury food scraps under bedding
4. Cover with more bedding
5. Harvest castings every 3-6 months

### Feeding Guide

| Worm Bin Size | Daily Food Scraps |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Small (1 gallon) | 1 cup |
| Medium (5 gallons) | 2 cups |
| Large (10 gallons) | 3-4 cups |

> **Pro Tip:** Start small. You can always upgrade as you get comfortable. A small bin under the sink handles most apartments' scraps.

## Method 2: Bokashi (Fastest, Most Versatile)

Bokashi uses beneficial microorganisms to pickle your food waste. It's the only indoor method that handles all food waste including meat and dairy.

### Pros
- Handles ALL food waste (including meat, dairy, cooked food)
- Fast - processes waste in 2-4 weeks
- Completely odorless
- Small bucket-sized system
- No maintenance between batches

### Cons
- Need to purchase bokashi bran
- Requires draining "bokashi tea" every 2-3 days
- Must bury/pot in soil after pickling (needs outdoor space or community garden)
- More "hands-on" than worm bin

### Getting Started

**Supplies Needed:**
- [Bokashi Bucket](https://www.amazon.com) - $35-50
- [Bokashi Bran](https://www.amazon.com) - $15 (lasts 6+ months)
- Spray bottle (for diluted bokashi tea)

**Basic Setup:**
1. Add food scraps to bucket (1-2 inches)
2. Sprinkle bokashi bran generously
3. Cover tightly between additions
4. Drain liquid every 2-3 days
5. Let ferment 2-4 weeks
6. Bury in soil or add to compost pile

### Bokashi Tea

The liquid drained from your bokashi bucket is concentrated fertilizer:

- **For plants:** Dilute 1:100 (1 tablespoon per gallon)
- **For drains:** Pour undiluted to clean pipes
- **Storage:** Must use within 2 weeks or freeze

> **Pro Tip:** Keep a small bokashi bucket in your freezer until collection day - stops smells completely!

## Method 3: Electric Composter (Most Expensive, Easiest)

Electric composters like the [Vitamix FoodCycler](https://www.amazon.com) or [Cinder](https://www.amazon.com) dry and process food waste in hours.

### Pros
- Truly plug-and-play
- Processes meat and dairy
- Odorless
- Produces ready-to-use compost in 24 hours
- No special maintenance

### Cons
- Expensive ($200-500)
- Small capacity (fits 1-2 days of scraps)
- Uses electricity
- Limited capacity for large families

### Best For
- Apartments where other methods aren't possible
- People who travel frequently
- Those wanting lowest effort option

## Problem Solving

### Smelly Worm Bin

**Cause:** Usually too wet, too much food, or wrong foods
**Solutions:**
- Add dry bedding (newspaper, cardboard)
- Reduce feeding
- Freeze scraps before adding (reduces moisture)
- Remove any rotting food immediately

### Fruit Flies

**Solutions:**
- Bury food under bedding
- Freeze scraps before adding
- Add a layer of dry bedding on top
- Use a lid

### Bokashi Not Pickling

**Signs:** Mold, foul smell, no "pickle" smell
**Cause:** Not enough bokashi bran
**Solution:** Use more bran next time

### Slow Decomposition

**Normal for worm bins** - takes 3-6 months
**For bokashi** - add more bran, ensure lid is sealed

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does apartment composting smell?

No! Worm farms and bokashi systems, when properly maintained, have no smell. Worm bins smell earthy. Bokashi has a sweet "pickle" smell. Any bad smells indicate problems.

### Can I compost year-round in my apartment?

Yes! Worm bins need indoor temperatures. In winter, keep your bin away from cold windows. Bokashi and electric composters work year-round regardless of temperature.

### What do I do with finished compost?

**Worm castings:** Mix into potting soil (20% by volume)
**Bokashi:** Bury in outdoor space or community garden, or add to potted plants in tiny amounts

### Can I compost without outdoor space?

Yes, worm castings can be used in potted plants - just mix into your potting soil. Bokashi must be buried, so it's only for those with occasional garden access.

### How much can I compost?

| Method | Weekly Capacity |
|--------|-----------------|
| Small worm bin | 1-2 lbs |
| Medium worm bin | 3-5 lbs |
| 5-gallon bokashi | 5-10 lbs |
| Electric composter | 3-5 lbs |

### Is composting in apartments legal?

Check your lease, but typically yes. Unlike outdoor composting, indoor systems don't attract pests when maintained properly.

## Conclusion

Apartment composting is completely achievable and incredibly rewarding. The key is choosing the right method:

- **Worm farming** - Best overall for most apartments, produces amazing fertilizer
- **Bokashi** - Best for meat/dairy and those with garden access
- **Electric** - Best for zero-effort option

Start with whatever fits your space and budget. Every pound of scraps you divert from the landfill is a small victory.
