# How to Grow Tomatoes on a Balcony

**Fresh, sun-ripened tomatoes from your balcony are absolutely achievable.** After helping thousands of apartment gardeners grow their own tomatoes, we've refined the perfect system for small-space success.

The key is choosing the right varieties, using adequate containers, and providing consistent care. This guide covers everything you need to grow 20-50 pounds of tomatoes from a single balcony.

> **Best Tomato Variety for Balconies:** [Tiny Tim Cherry Tomato](https://www.amazon.com) - Compact, prolific, perfect for containers. Grows just 12 inches tall!

---

## Why Grow Tomatoes on Your Balcony?

### The Benefits

- **Fresh, vine-ripened flavor** - Nothing compares
- **Significant savings** - 50 lbs of tomatoes = $150+ value
- **Satisfaction of growing your own food**
- **Beautiful plants** - Tomatoes are ornamental too
- **Pollinator friendly** - Attracts bees to your balcony

### The Challenges (And Solutions)

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| Limited space | Choose compact/cascade varieties |
| Wind on high balconies | Use windbreaks, stake securely |
| Weight limits | Use lightweight potting mix |
| Sunlight requirements | Ensure 6+ hours direct sun |
| Watering needs | Use self-watering containers |

## Soil and Planting

### The Perfect Tomato Soil Mix

```
4 parts high-quality potting soil
1 part perlite
1 part compost
1/4 cup garden lime (per 5 gallons)
1/4 cup slow-release fertilizer (tomato food)
```

### Planting Depth

Tomatoes are unique - they grow roots along their stems. **Plant deeply:**

1. Fill container with soil mix to 2 inches from top
2. Remove lower leaves from seedling
3. Plant so 2/3 of stem is buried
4. Fill in around stem
5. Water deeply until it drains from bottom
6. Add soil if it settles

> **Secret Technique:** Lay the plant on its side in a trench if it's leggy. It will straighten up and grow extra roots along the buried stem!

## Care Through the Season

### Watering

Tomatoes need consistent moisture. Inconsistent watering causes:
- Blossom end rot
- Cracking
- Blossom drop

**Watering Rules:**
- Water deeply when top 2 inches of soil are dry
- Usually every 1-2 days in hot weather
- Water at soil level, not on leaves
- Morning watering is best

### Fertilizing

Tomatoes are heavy feeders. Feed every 2 weeks:

| Growth Stage | Fertilizer Type |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Early (first 4 weeks) | High nitrogen (for leaves) |
| Flowering/Fruiting | Low nitrogen, high phosphorus/potassium |

**Our Recommended Tomato Fertilizers:**
- [Espoma Tomato-tone](https://www.amazon.com) - Organic, gentle
- [Miracle-Gro Tomato Food](https://www.amazon.com) - Fast-acting

### Staking and Support

Even compact tomatoes need support:

- **Tomato cage** - Easiest for containers
- **Stake and tie** - Saves space
- **String trellis** - For small spaces

Install support AT planting time to avoid damaging roots later.

## Common Problems and Solutions

### Blossom End Rot

**What it looks like:** Dark, sunken spot on bottom of fruit
**Cause:** Calcium deficiency from inconsistent watering
**Solution:** Mulch, water consistently, add calcium supplement

### Yellow Leaves

**What it looks like:** Leaves turn yellow, often bottom of plant first
**Cause:** Usually nitrogen deficiency or overwatering
**Solution:** Feed with nitrogen fertilizer, check drainage

### No Fruit

**What it looks like:** Plant grows but no tomatoes
**Cause:** Temperature extremes or poor pollination
**Solution:** Wait for warmer temps, hand-pollinate with gentle shake

### Leggy Growth

**What it looks like:** Long stems, few leaves, weak plant
**Cause:** Not enough light
**Solution:** Move to sunnier spot or accept smaller harvest

### Cracking Fruit

**What it looks like:** Tomatoes split open
**Cause:** Inconsistent watering - too much water after drought
**Solution:** Mulch, water consistently

## Harvesting

### When to Pick

- **Cherry tomatoes:** When fully colored and slightly soft
- **Slicing tomatoes:** When fully colored, slightly firm
- **Green tomatoes:** Can ripen indoors on sunny windowsill

### How to Harvest

- Twist gently - ripe tomatoes separate easily
- Use clean scissors for clean cuts
- Don't refrigerate ripe tomatoes - loses flavor
- Pick regularly to encourage more production

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I grow tomatoes on a north-facing balcony?

No. Tomatoes need 6+ hours of direct sunlight daily. A south or west-facing balcony is essential. Consider shade-tolerant vegetables instead like lettuce or herbs.

### How many tomato plants can I grow on my balcony?

For a typical apartment balcony (6-8 feet):
- 2-3 cherry tomato plants OR
- 1-2 slicing tomato plants

### Why do my tomatoes flower but not fruit?

Temperature is usually the culprit. Tomatoes won't set fruit when nights are below 55°F or days above 90°F. Also, very high nitrogen causes leafy growth at the expense of fruit.

### Should I prune tomato plants in containers?

For cherry tomatoes - minimal pruning. For slicing tomatoes - remove "suckers" (growth between main stem and branches) for larger fruit. Never remove more than 1/3 of the plant.

### Can I reuse tomato container soil next year?

Only if plants were healthy. Mix old soil 50/50 with fresh potting mix and add fresh compost. Tomatoes are heavy feeders and deplete soil significantly.
